Indian Constitution

The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the country. It lays down the framework defining fundamental political principles, procedures, powers, and duties of government institutions.

  • It was adopted on 26 November 1949.
  • It came into effect on 26 January 1950.
  • It is the longest written constitution in the world.
  • It defines the boundaries of the political community.
  • It commits states to social, economic, and developmental goals.
